6 Months of Book Blogging

September 5th, 2013 | Posted by Alix in The Reading Life | Thoughts & Musings

Today marks my 6 month anniversary as a book blogger! I can hardly believe that it’sÂ already andÂ only been 6 months since Paola asked me to reviewÂ The AshfordÂ AffairÂ which led to my officially joining A Novel Idea as a full time reviewer.Â It’s been a crazy adventure ever since!

From Getty Images

I’ve been online in some form or another (whether running a fan site, personal blog, private blog, community moderator) since I was a young teenager in the early 90s (I refuse to do the math because it has been that long). Yes I remember the days of dialup and having a limited amount of hours per month of internet connection time. My first site was built in an Angelfire WYSIWYG editor and I was so proud when I moved on to Geocities’Â WYSIWYG! Eventually through the awesome (at the time) site LissaÂ Explains It AllÂ I learned how to code basic HTML websites. I even had a domain where I had a bunch of fan sites and even hostees! While I was never a “big” name in the online worlds I traveled in, I found a place for myself and made friendships that endure to this day.

Then I started university and my online presence pretty much disappeared.Â I had a public personal blog that I had been keeping for years and where I did some pretty kick ass design/coding considering my next to zero knowledge but decided to let it go. I had kept a private, restricted blog going but shut down my public facing online life. I loved being online and creating/managing sites but I had lost interest in the subjects of my fan sites and I’m also a very private person so having a personal blog was never the right fit. Flash forward 6 years! I found myself with free time thanks to a new job and feeling the pull of going back to blogging. I started a lifestyle blog. I put so much into it but was never really able to do what I wanted with it. I had all these grand ideas but by then, HTML had progressed so far that I felt lost in it and I simply had different priorities (ie.: choosing to spend time with friends and family vs figuring out coding). I couldn’t keep up with regular posting and it just wasn’t a satisfying experience. While all of this was happening, I had joined an online book club that included Paola.

I’m gonna back track a little now. I’ve always loved reading. It’s alway played a huge part in my life. I was so excited when I finally finished uni and realized I had time again toÂ read things I wanted to! Over the years I’ve been in 3 RL book clubs and 2 online ones (including the now defunct one where I met Paola). But for some reason, I had never thought about blogging about it (even though I ran the site for one of my RL book clubs). I read book blogs and loved books but I never made the link that it could be something I could do and enjoy until Paola asked me to join A Novel Idea. I haven’t looked back since.

From Getty Images

Book blogging has been a challenge, I won’t lie. Those first couple of months when I started using NetGalley to request ARCs, I would request all the books I found interesting and found myself more then once overwhelmed with having to read and write reviews for books that I had time to read. And yes, there were a few books which I didn’t end up reviewing either because I didn’t have time, I didn’t find them interesting or missed the archive date.Â Over the months I’ve learned to better balance ARCs, my TBR pile and reviews. I’m far from feeling like I’ve found the perfect balance but it’s not as overwhelming as it used to be. Paola has been awesome at supporting me through this and telling it’s okay to not review a book.Â I’ve also struggled with the fact that I don’t review genres that are as popular here… This isn’t a complaint; I full intended on converting all of you to contemporary YA (if I can convert Paola, I can convert anyone), New Adult and Romance & Chick Lit!

The best part of book blogging has been by far getting to know more people, especially Paola! She is such an awesome lady and I wouldn’t be doing this if it weren’t for her support. Blogging isÂ hard in a good and rewarding way and doing it with someone else makes it more fun and less stressful! I’m also slowly getting to know more of you via Twitter, which is great. I’ve also gotten the chance to learn about books I might have never heard of before and be able to discuss so many various things with all of you, most book related, some not (*coughROYALScough*). Book blogging has been a creative outlet in a way for me and has become not just a hobby but something I truly love doing and hope to grow more into.

I’ve also set goals for myself as a book blogger for the next 6 months:

  • Continue to work on finding a better balance between ARCs and my TBR pile. I’ve felt like I’ve really neglected some books I’ve been meaning to read in favour of ARCs and I want to be able to enjoy both my reading lists equally.
  • Work on my reviewing style and voice. Paola likes calling me the “classy” reviewer cause I don’t tend to ramble as much as her (FYI I don’t believe that at all!) but I’m not sure if that’s me or not. I’m still searching for my reviewing voice but I feel like I’m making good progress!
  • Not be bothered by the fact that my posts get no or few comments. It’s rough putting so much time into something and not getting much feedback or comments. It’s something Paola has been great at reminding me isn’t the end of the world.
  • Setting aside more time to blog in the week like if this was a job (a job that is totally awesome!).
  • Get better acquainted with the awesome book blogger network A Novel Idea has! I know you guys know Paola pretty well; she’s more on Twitter then I am and is the commenting queen! I’m quite shy (more so online then in RL strangely enough) so I get intimated (which I know is crazy since you’re all so nice and friendly!).
  • Generally get more involved in the book blogging community. I’ll be participating in my first ever solo event in November, the Sci-fi month event hosted at Rinn Reads! I’m super excited (and a little bit scared – hahaha)!

I’m looking forward to what the future holds for me as a book blogger! I’m hoping to eventually attend BEA andÂ finally meet PaolaÂ (yes we have never met in person before)! I hope this post gave you guys some insight into who I am a little more since I know I’m the ~mysterious~ one here. :)

What about you? How long have you been blogging? What have you loved and found difficult about it? What are your goals as a book blogger? Got any questions for me?
