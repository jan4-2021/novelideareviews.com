A-Z Bookish Survey

August 15th, 2013 | Posted by Paola in Features

We’re a little late to the party, but this A-Z Bookish Survey from Jamie @ The Perpetual Page-Turner was just too fun to miss out on! In the spirit of all those good old-fashioned Xanga/MySpace surveys that were so popular in the days of our youth, here’s an (alphabetical) account of our reading habits :) If you did the survey too, please leave us a link so we can come read your post!

Author you’ve read the most books from:

Paola - Laura Ingalls Wilder. I’ve been a Little House fan since childhood and own multiple dog-eared, practically-falling-apart copies of the books in the series. Close contenders would be Robin McKinley, J.K. Rowling, Diana Wynne Jones and Sarah Dessen.

Alix - I would it’s Jane Austen since I read all of her novels (from first published to last!) back-to-back after receiving the Complete Works compilation book in high school! Closely followed by Henning Mankel (excellent Swedish crime novels!), Meg Cabot (I’ll basically read anything by her and practically have), J.K. Rowling (just missing her pen name book to have read all her works!), Ann M. Martin (The Baby-Sitters Club!),

Best sequel ever:

Paola - Oh man, this is a tough question. I don’t usually like sequels at all, especially not the ones that are Book 2 in a trilogy. I guess I’d have to say Froi of the Exiles by Melina Marchetta, because going back to Lumatere could never be wrong. Err, except I never actually left…

Alix - Catching Fire is one of those rare 2nd-in-a-series books that outdid the first and final book for me. It is my favourite book in the series. I devoured it and for me, it was best the written, most powerful and feeling causing book in the Hunger Games series.

Currently reading:

Paola - Um, like 12 books at once? I’m incapable of selecting one book to read at a time. Right now I’m juggling Shadows on the Moon by Zoe Marriott (AMAZING), Fangirl by Rainbow Rowell (ALSO AMAZING) and Of Beast & Beauty by Stacey Jay (a truly unique take on the Beauty and the Beast fairytale).

Alix - Nothing… I KNOW! My life has been so crazy for the 2 weeks that I didn’t start another book after finished my last. Since I’m always reading a bunch of books at once I’m technically readingÂ TheÂ ExiledÂ Queen by Cinda Williams Chima and A Feast for Crows by George R.R. Martin but haven’t touched them in the last couple of weeks. I’m super looking forward to starting Fangirl this week though!

Drink of choice while reading:

Paola - An iced mocha. Sooooo good.

Alix - COFFEEEEEEEE!!!! *cough*

E-Reader or physical book?

Paola - Hmm, it depends. Some books just NEED to be read in their physical format and for some reason are more satisfying that way. But most of the time I prefer ebooks because they’re convenient, portable, and I can highlight the shit out of them. (I’ve been known to take photos of pages in books with my phone because I can’t highlight library copies…)

Alix - E-Reader mostly because of practically reasons. As I mentioned in our ReadingÂ Overseas interview, it’s easier for me to get ebooks in English then physical books (unless I buy them). I do still prefer physical books but my e-reader just makes it easier and also very much appeals to my book ADD! That being said, I was quite distraught that I didn’t have a physical book with me when my Kobo died during my commute awhile back! #lessonlearnedthehardway

Fictional character you probably would’ve actually dated in high school:

Paola - Oh geez. – insert name of self-centered douchebag character here – I was kind of a magnet for boy problems in high school. I wish I could say they’d all been Dessen boys, haha. (A little Wes or Owen would’ve been nice!!)

Alix - Considering I didn’t date any guys in high school this a easy/hard one to answer… Though knowing my track record with guys post-HS, I would say that unfortunately it would be one of those artistic/musical type guys that end up breaking your heart. P

Glad you gave this book a chance:

Paola - Speaking of Wes, my answer for this one would have to be The Truth About Forever by Sarah Dessen. Totally not into contemporary, but this was the book that made me more willing to try the genre.

Alix - In general it would Prelude to Foundation byÂ Isaac Asimov. A good friend recommend it to me after I found myself for the first time in my life with spare time to read. I had never read anything sci-fi before and although I loved sci-fi tv shows, I thought the books were too “geeky” for me. Boy was I wrong! I absolutely lovedÂ Prelude to Foundation and it opened up the door to me to read more sci-fi novels since, which some are now all-time favourites of mine. YA-wise, it would beÂ The Hunger Games. Before reading it, I had only dabbled in YA as an adult; I had read Twilight and the Pretty Little Liars series and while I had enjoyed them, it was The Hunger Games that made me realize just how good and impactful YA can be and made me seek out the genre actively in my reading life.

Hidden Gem book:

Paola - Vessel by Sarah Beth Durst, hands down. This book is amazing. The heroine is one of my favorites of all time — she’s got just enough sass, survival instinct, and wit. I want to be Liyana when I grow up. If you’re a fantasy fan and haven’t given this a try, WHY NOT?? Cause I’m pretty sure I told you to go get it when I wrote my review. *glares*

Alix - The Secret Society Girl book series byÂ Diana Peterfreund. You want to know what New Adult is all about? And how it should read when it’s done well? Then this is the series for you! There are 4 books in the series and they are ALL so good! Amy is a flawed, awesome and related college student navigating the waters of growing up and figuring out post college life WITHIN THE CONTEXT OF A SECRET SOCIETY! How can you NOT want to read that? Witty dialogues, a nice mix of real life drama and a little over-the-top drama (in a good way), hot guys, amazing friends, exotic locations! This series has it all!

Important moment in your reading life:

Paola - When I learned how to walk to the library by myself in third grade. I’d go every weekend and sometimes after school, just to sit in the stacks and read whatever I got my hands on. That was the first time I ever realized how much of an escape reading could be, both physically and mentally.

Alix -Â When I realized I could read in English. I know this might sound weird but having grown up going to school in French, I was never formally thought to read English beyond my mother reading English books to me. Then one night during story time, I started reading a page and both my mom and I were kinda shocked to piece together that somehow along the way, I had learned from her how to read. This opened up the world of English literature and being able to read in both English and French has been amazing for me!

Just finished:

Paola - Belle Epoque by Elizabeth Ross. But my review doesn’t go up until next Friday, so I’ll keep quiet about it for now~

Alix - Rush Me by Allison Parr. An excellent NA book I read in one sitting (oops!). Needless to say I loved it but to know why you’re going to have to read my review…

Kind of books you won’t read:

Paola - I’m willing to try most anything, but I don’t read very much heavy science fiction and tend to avoid crime novels.

Alix - I’m willing to give almost any genre a try although being a scaredy-cat, I tend to avoid full on horror novels (sorry Stephen King!).

Longest book you’ve read:

Paola - Ugh, The Wise Man’s Fear by Patrick Rothfuss. It was like 900+ pages. And while I loved the first book, this one just did NOT work for me. While we’re on the topic, though, another fantasy must-read is The Name of the Wind. Trust me, with the first installment, the length is well worth it.

Alix - A Storm of Swords by George R.R. Martin at 1,074 pages! It took me to few hundred pages to get into it but once I did, boy did I ever! I have read much shorter books that have felt longer to read then this one did. Worth every page to get to the devastating end (if you watched season 3 of Game of Thrones you know what I’m talking about).

Major book hangover because of:

Paola - FINNIKIN OF THE ROCK. I ran around my house screaming/sobbing. Then I ran into my husband’s office and spent some time clinging to his leg, still screaming/sobbing. (Not kidding, you can ask him about the time he sat there looking very bewildered while I waved Finnikin in his face and declared that my life was over.) The original FBF (Facepunched By Feels) book.

Alix -Â The Sea of Tranquility. Another book that I pretty read in one sitting because it wasÂ that good, I ended up finishing to read in the middle of the night and couldn’t fall asleep because of the emotions I still had in me from the book. It took me days to process everything and write my review of it. It’s a book that I still find myself thinking about all these months later.

Number of bookcases you own:

Paola - Officially? Two. Unofficially? Impossible to count. This would be because I tend to pile books on most available surfaces in my house, thus transforming them into impromptu bookshelves.

Alix - 3 full bookshelves plus another 4 that are a combo of nicknacks and books…

One book you have read multiple times:

Paola - The Last Unicorn by Peter S. Beagle. It’s my go-to book for the times when I need something comforting, magical and heartbreaking all at the same time.

Alix -Â Pride & Prejudice. You can’t go wrong with an Austen classic.

Preferred place to read:

Paola - In bed. Especially if it’s raining or in the middle of winter when I have an actual excuse to use my electric blanket.

Alix - In bed or in the bath.

Paola - “Our troubles are but mayflies, rising and falling between the turn of dawn and dusk. And when they are gone to the houses of memory, you and I will remain, Yukiko.” (From Stormdancer by Jay Kristoff. Excuse me while I go sob into a pillow for a while. And if you’re wondering why I didn’t choose anything from the Lumatere Chronicles, it’s because all my favorites from that series are also massive spoilers.)

Reading Regret:

Paola - I know I’m probably supposed to talk about a book I regretted reading, but honestly my biggest bookish regret right now is not being able to re-read old favorites anymore ever since I transitioned to full scale blogging. I just don’t have the time. I’m working hard to clear out all my ARCs and library books within 1-2 months so that I can have a break at the end of the year. And then I’m going on an epic re-reading binge!

Alix - I don’t think I really have any… Like Paola, I’m definitely still trying to find the balance between re-reading favourites/reading for myself/reading and reviewing for the blog.

Series you started and need to finish (all books are out in the series):

Paola - The Seven Realms series by Cinda Williams Chima. Alix and I read the first book, The Demon King, as our #2PersonBookClub pick for June and we both fell in love with it. I desperately want to know what happens next, but my perpetual landslide of TBR books keeps getting between me and the second installment! /cries

Alix -Â Divergent series! Ok so I know it says a series that all the books are out and it’s done but there isn’t a series I’ve stopped reading mi-way through that isn’t intentional and the last Divergent book is coming out in October so I’m gonna pretend this counts. It’s one of my favourite books but I haven’t read the second book, Insurgent, yet. I think I suffer from a case of having loved the first book so much, I’m afraid the rest of the series won’t live up to my expectations.

Three of your all-time favorite books:

Paola - A Tree Grows In Brooklyn by Betty Smith, Sabriel by Garth Nix, and Rose Daughter by Robin McKinley.

Alix - Pride & Prejudice by Jane Austen, The Alchemist by Paulo Coelho and Harry Potter by JK Rowling.

Unapologetic fangirl for:

Paola - Various Harry Potter-isms, anything Lumatere Chronicles (obviously), and Celaena Sardothien!

Alix -Â Harry Potter,Â Nancy Drew & the Hardy Boys Super Mysteries, Jane Austen!

Very excited for this release more than all the others:

Paola - This is a tie between Crown of Midnight by Sarah J. Maas (SOON! VERY SOON!!) and Cress by Marissa Meyer. Even a few weeks away from release, I’d still fight someone for an ARC of Crown of Midnight. And I would DEFINITELY fight someone for an ARC of Cress, considering the agonizing wait between now and the 2014 publication date. /sobs

Worst bookish habit:

Paola - (OCCASIONALLY!) peeking at the last page. So sue me.

Alix - Definitely read the last page (or couple of pages) of a book…

X Marks The Spot: Start at the top left of your shelf and pick the 27th book:

Paola - The Dalemark Quartet by Diana Wynne Jones. I have the omnibus edition containing all four books. I absolutely love this series and I think it’s a true testament to the versatility of the author; this is epic high fantasy, which contrasts with her more lighthearted fantasy works such as Howl’s Moving Castle or the Chrestomanci books. She does both variations so well, which is why I love Diana Wynne Jones so much.

Paola - The Ocean At the End of the Lane by Neil Gaiman, which I read all in one sitting from midnight until 2am. On a weeknight. Because I’m a wild, reckless human being. And also because this book was extremely good, of course.
