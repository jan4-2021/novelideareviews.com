Chapter Seven

Oh snap! Mrs. Clay, are you now gunning for Cousin Elliott instead of Sir Walter??! You gold-digging minx, you! Not that this worked since Cousin E decided to go walking in the rain with Anne. Which, of course, is when Wentworth finally decides to come back into our story! (A wild Wentworth appears! SNORT) After seeing this other dude taking walks in the rain with Anne, Wentworth decides that he ALSO wants to do this. Plus he wants to show off his shiny new umbrella. But Cousin E interrupts the conversation, and a stroll with Anne is not in the cards for Wentworth.

As the days pass, Wentworth sightings continue to take place all around Bath. Even Lady Russell takes some time to stare at him. She covers this up by pretending to be staring at some really snazzy drawing room curtains instead. Smooth, Lady R. Anne says nothing about this but does make some private commentary on “the elegant stupidity of private parties.” Because she’s Anne, and sometimes snarky, even if she doesn’t ever say these things out loud.

By the end of the chapter, everyone is in full freak-out mode over an upcoming concert. Anne doesn’t really want to go… until she realizes that Wentworth might be there. #Gamechanger

Classics Retold: Persuasion: the movie

September 27th, 2013 | Posted by Alix in Classic | Classics Retold Project - (18 Comments)

About Classics Retold | Our Intro Post

Please excuse us while A Novel Idea takes a little break from talking about books to talk about… a movie!Â I know!

There are probably as many movie/TV adaptions of Jane Austen’s works as their are book adaptions. And I have all 99% of them as any true Austenite has (yes I have even see the Bollywood adaption of P&P but that’s a story for another time). The BBC has been my Austen dealer since I was a teen and they continued to feed my addiction a few years ago when they readapted most of Austen’s works including Persuasion! Now there is a 1995 adaptation of Persuasion that is good. But the thing is, Wentworth is not attractive (yes I am that superficial but how can I not be after they gave us Colin Firth jumping into a lake!). Luckily 2007 came around and the BBC gave me us adaption starring the very handsome Rupert Penry-Jones!

Whether you are an Austenite or have no interest in ever reading her books, I still think you should check out this adaption of Persuasion. Aside from the fact that it’s adapted from a literary work, Persuasion is a great story. Still not convinced? Intrigued?? Just curious why I love it so much??? Well good news!

Here are my reasons why you MUST watch the 2007 adaption of Persuasion:

The story is actually very well adapted. Sure lots of stuff it cut out but this version clocks in at 90 minutes so it’s normal. If you aren’t super familiar or at all familiar with the story, you will never be able to tell!


WENTWORTH IS HOT. LIKE SERIOUSLY VERY, VERY HOT. Yes I am that superficial. No I don’t care. LOOK AT THAT FACE! Sure I pictured Wentworth with dark hair BUT WHATEVER.Â Rupert Penry-Jones is PERFECT as Wentworth. Not only he is super good looking but he’s great Wentworth too! He’s able to capture the him perfectly.

Anne and Wentworth’s love story is truly beautiful. These actors have crazy chemistry and combined that with a story where you just can’t help but cheer on the guy and the girl getting back together makes for a wonderful way to spend a couple of hours. You feel Anne’s regret at letting herself have been persuaded by her family and friends to reject Wentworth, and you understand why Wentworth is cool towards her (oh dudes and their fragile egos). You cannot but help but cheer for these two crazies kids to get their issues resolved and make out.

OH THE ANGST!Â PersuasionÂ is full of angst: from Anne over Wentworth and his possible engagement to another woman, to Wentworth still being bitter about Anne’s rejection, I think Austen might have called itÂ AngstÂ (if that word had existed in the 19th century. You get plenty ofÂ those looks: Anne sneaking a glance at Wentworth (I lost track of the number of times they have those looks!), Wentworth sneaking a glance at Anne, Anne’s inner monologue about being “over him” and crying over the past of her diary. If you like angst, this delivers x 100.
