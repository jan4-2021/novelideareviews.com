CHAPTER FIVE: MARGUERITE

In the previous chapter, jolly innkeeper Mr. Jellyband finally decided to tell everyone that more visitors were expected: Sir Percy Blakeney and his wife, Lady Blakeney. (SURPRISE!!!) This turns out to be totally awkward because the conversation at dinner just veered from the Comtesse de Tournay sobbing hysterically over her soup to suddenly talking a whole lot of smack about Lady Blakeney, who was once Marguerite St. Just — a former French actress/socialite whose testimony sent an entire family to the guillotine. While the Comtesse has a pretty low opinion of this Marguerite, most everyone else can’t believe Lady B would ever do such a thing. Suzanne de Tournay even has fond memories of going to school with Marguerite. (And if you already don’t remember who all these people are, backtrack to Part 1! THERE WILL BE A TEST. Not really.)

In the resulting mess of confusion and awkwardness that ensues, the group votes to hide the refugees upstairs while Sir Percy and Marguerite are at the inn. Great plan, except for the part where they totally failed at doing so. The Blakeneys arrive at The Fisherman’s Rest and promptly encounter the de Tournays before they’re even halfway up the stairs. (Insert ‘fashionable oaths’ from Lord Tony here. LOL.) Within moments, the Comtesse has managed to gravely insult Marguerite Blakeney by insinuating that she’s a two-faced snitch and probably an enemy of France. (Well, this is what I gathered from the conversation, anyway.) All who witness this exchange are utterly mortified.

The Comtesse forbids Suzanne from interacting with Marguerite, but she doesn’t listen and gives the lady a big hug anyway. Definitely the Disney Princess thing to do.

In a Nutshell: Basically, this is a story of a charming time in history (1792) when the guillotine was the spectator sport of choice in France. Rich, aristocratic people are being decapitated left and right, and not all of them deserve it. Their only champions are the dashing British gentlemen who call themselves The Scarlet Pimpernel. They undertake daring rescue missions for the sake of Justice. (CAPITAL J OKAY???)
