Paola’s back!

I had a nice long break from blogging in November while I was working on NaNoWriMo. My brain is very, very tired, but I did miss being here. Recaps or Forecasts?

So for a few months now we’ve been trying to post both an end of the month recap AND a monthly forecast telling you about upcoming highlights on the blog. The truth is, doing both is rather exhausting, even when they only have to be done once a month. Alix and I both work full time and it can be difficult to work these in, especially when the book haul can be a pretty big post. We may only keep one of these posts a month, but we’d like to ask our readers which you prefer: do you like recaps of what we’ve been up to all month, or do you prefer to know what’s coming up in the future?? Please leave us a comment or hit us up on Twitter to let us know your thoughts!

We never envisioned ourselves getting to this point in our blogging career, but Alix and I have made the decision to close all review submissions. Our schedule is pretty much booked for reviews, events and planned features all the way up until the end of 2013 (which we’re exceedingly grateful for & excited about!), and there is just no feasible way we can keep up with review requests. On that note, a huge thank you to the authors who have submitted their books to us — we are so honored and really regret that we can’t take on more requests at this time. Our review policy has been updated to reflect the change and we’ve added a form that will put future submissions on our waiting list for consideration. Whew!!!

#2PersonBookClub – September 2013

Every month we choose one book for both of us to read and discuss together. This month’s selection is For Darkness Shows the Stars by Diana Peterfreund, a retelling of Jane Austen’s Persuasion. As we mentioned above, we’re participating in the Classics Retold event and signed up for Persuasion, so this is one the adaptations we’ll be covering. We figured, why not be super clever and combine #2PB with a book we’ll be reading for the event anyway? (I know, we’re too smart for our own good~)

It’s been several generations since a genetic experiment gone wrong caused the Reduction, decimating humanity and giving rise to a Luddite nobility who outlawed most technology.

Elliot North has always known her place in this world. Four years ago Elliot refused to run away with her childhood sweetheart, the servant Kai, choosing duty to her family’s estate over love. Since then the world has changed: a new class of Post-Reductionists is jumpstarting the wheel of progress, and Elliot’s estate is foundering, forcing her to rent land to the mysterious Cloud Fleet, a group of shipbuilders that includes renowned explorer Captain Malakai Wentforth–an almost unrecognizable Kai. And while Elliot wonders if this could be their second chance, Kai seems determined to show Elliot exactly what she gave up when she let him go.

But Elliot soon discovers her old friend carries a secret–one that could change their society . . . or bring it to its knees. And again, she’s faced with a choice: cling to what she’s been raised to believe, or cast her lot with the only boy she’s ever loved, even if she’s lost him forever.

Inspired by Jane Austen’s Persuasion, For Darkness Shows the Stars is a breathtaking romance about opening your mind to the future and your heart to the one person you know can break it. (From Goodreads)

A Novel Idea Turns 5!

Believe it or not, this little corner of the internet has been around since 2008! For the first four years of its existence, A Novel Idea was more of a personal blog and I never promoted it as a full-fledged book blog. And then I decided to come back and make book blogging a bigger part of my life, which is honestly one of the best decisions I’ve ever made. Thanks to this blog, I’ve met so many amazing people, become better friends with my fabulous co-blogger Alix, and now I pretty much ALWAYS have someone to talk about books with! In honor of our 5th birthday, we’re unveiling a shiny redesign of the blog on Monday, August 5th. We hope you find some time to stop by and tell us what you think! (Psst… we may be giving away some books…) While we’re on the subject, if we’re down for maintenance a lot over the next few days, don’t panic. AWESOMENESS IS HAPPENING.
