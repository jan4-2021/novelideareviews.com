Crown of Midnight

September 2nd, 2013 | Posted by Paola in Fantasy | Review | YA Fiction

TITLE: Crown of Midnight (Throne of Glass, #2)

AUTHOR: Sarah J. Maas

GENRE: YA Fantasy

PUBLISHED: August 2013, Bloomsbury Walker; Hardcover, 420 pages

PRECEDED BY: Throne of Glass, Throne of Glass Novellas

Celaena Sardothien is now the King’s Champion, sworn to be the hand that brings death to his enemies. But although Celaena may have declared fealty to the King of Adarlan — the same man who robbed her of kin and country — she is far from loyal to him. There has never been a time when she blindly followed orders, and now is no exception. This is a secret she keeps from even those closest to her, one that could lead to the loss of everything she has managed to gain since Prince Dorian and his Captain of the Guard came to take her from the salt mines of Endovier. What will Adarlan’s Assassin be willing to sacrifice, and where do her true loyalties lie?

*brain explodes* I CAN’T REVIEW THIS BOOK. I CAN’T. Okay, maybe I can… but it’s gonna take A LOT of cake.

You know that horrible little thing called Second Book Syndrome? Well, none of it is happening here. This is what the second book in a series should look like, people. Crown of Midnight takes all the certified badassery of the first book and multiplies it tenfold. Everyone has secrets they would kill to keep and there isn’t a single moment when you’re not being beaten over the head with high intensity feels. Everything I loved about Throne of Glass took on new dimensions. People I thought I couldn’t love more ended up reaching for my heart and crushing it in their fists. HOW AM I SUPPOSED TO WAIT FOR THE THIRD BOOK???

Ahem. Okay. Anyway, what I really want to say about Crown of Midnight is that it completely stole the show from its predecessor. Celaena returns fiercer than ever (and with an even deeper appreciation for dessert). I love how all her experiences continue to shape her, creating a character that is constantly evolving. Love, loss, rage and revenge are such shallow words when it comes to Celaena; it’s like you can’t just describe her with plain English. Seriously, I can barely articulate my feelings right now. She’s one of my favorite characters ever. And Chaol? Absolutely PERFECT. His development here is spot on. I expected the conflict to intensify on his end, especially after everything that happened in the first book. But all that heavy emotional stuff aside, I will always pick Chaol because he has such a keen understanding of exactly when a girl could use some chocolate hazelnut cake. FORREALS.

Dorian though, guys. I wasn’t really a Dorian fan when I read Throne of Glass, but I have to say that his character’s growth in book two was incredible. He gained so much depth. And I really, really, REALLY wanted to give him a hug. I never thought I’d end up with such an appreciation for Dorian, but now I do. Such is the power of Crown of Midnight.

Speaking of Dorian, I’m sure everyone knows that I will ship Celaena/Chaol up until the day I die, so I won’t inundate you with all my lovestruck babble here. I don’t know how to talk about these two without massive spoilers sneaking in, so I just won’t. Well, okay, maybe I’ll say ONE thing, which I will hide under the spoiler cut below. Stay the hell away from this if you haven’t read the book yet!

Hang on, have to stop and take deep yoga breaths to calm my shattered nerves. /melodrama/

RATING: 5/5

I realize that this review wasn’t nearly as deep and insightful as you probably wanted to see from me, but I am simply unable to sound intelligent after reading something that involves Feels Facepunches of this magnitude. If you want a well-written, completely coherent review of Crown of Midnight, go visit Charlene at Bookish Whimsy. She’s way more levelheaded about this than I am. Um, but if you’re in need of someone to sob hysterically with, I’m your girl! I’m heartbroken, truthfully, but in a good way. This is one series that will be breaking my heart and putting it back together again all the way to the end.
