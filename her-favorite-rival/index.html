Her Favorite Rival

September 4th, 2013 | Posted by Alix in ARC | Review | Romance

Sarah Mayberry strikes again in the best possible way. If you have any sort of preconceived notions as to what romance novels are (light/easy reading, fluffy content, no character dimension/development, lacklustre story progression) then you NEED to pick up this book (or practically any other title by Mayberry) to see how romance novel can be everything a fiction novel is plus hotness!

Anyway, back to Her Favorite Rival! If you’re a Mayberry fan, you’ll have heard about the free novella she released a precursor to this title called Her Favorite Temptation which first and quickly introduces us to Audrey, the main character in the story here. Audrey and Zach are coworkers and rivals up for the same promotion at their company. Although from a  privileged background, Audrey was had to work for everything she has and has made her way up through the ranks of the company. Zach is seemingly the golden boy born with a sliver spoon in his mouth but after being assigned on a project together, Audrey quickly learns that appearance can be deceiving.

The story did a great job of balancing Audrey (and Zach) desires to get ahead professionally. Often times romances have the woman needing to make a choice between her career and love but not here, this is very much a story about finding the balance between work and love and it being OK to be ambitious in your work life and want a personal life. Audrey makes no excuses for being a workaholic, isn’t seen a “less then” because she does and personally as a career oriented woman in her late twenties I loved this and could really related to it. I also really loved all of Audrey’s back story with her family. In  Her Favorite Temptation, we learn that Audrey made a mistake as a teen and has turned into the family’s black sheep. The dynamic of her relationship with her parents was heartbreaking and I was cheering for her to reconcile with her sister through out! I loved this aspect of her storyline so much, I wish we had had gotten more of it (and less of the description of the various tasks of Audrey’s office job). 

I found Zach to also be an interesting character. I didn’t see his past coming at all and it was a nice surprise. He was a great romantic lead and I love how one of the reasons he is so attracted to Audrey is her drive to excel at work. It’s, once again, refreshing to see a woman be able to be strong and successful and have that be a good thing (and not a thing to pity because her love life seemingly lacks). The only part of the book I enjoyed less was in relation to Zach and his past and the situation with his mother. Something about it just didn’t work for me but I was glad to see the conflict it caused between Audrey and Zach be quickly addressed and have them move past it.

The growing attraction between Audrey and Zach and eventual romance felt natural. It progressed organically and I never doubt for a minute why they were so into each other! I was cheering them on and once they did get together? It was very hot and very satisfying! They also had great banter through out the book which had me laughing out loud at times!

RATING: 4.5/5

Sarah Mayberry once again created wonderful, complex character set in a relatable story. Her Favorite Rival has everything that makes up a great story wrapped up with hotness. I definitely recommend you pick up this book even if you haven’t ever read a romance novel before!
