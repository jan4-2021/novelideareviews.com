It’s Our 5th Birthday! Redesign + Giveaway

August 5th, 2013 | Posted by Paola in Announcements | Giveaway

Five years ago (in ye olde 2008), this blog was just a sleepy little corner of the Internet filled with stacks of books. The stacks of books are still here — in fact, they’ve grown taller than we are — but A Novel Idea is so much more now than it was then. Thanks for the best, most beautiful year the blog has ever had. We’re hoping for many more. Happy 5th birthday, A Novel Idea! We’re celebrating with a redesign, a giveaway, and cupcakes for all!

I know that A Novel Idea will continue to grow and evolve, but one thing will never change: it’s always going to be a place to share our love of books and connect with others who love them as much as we do.

About the Design

Our new header was illustrated by Paola’s extremely talented sister, who took a pencil sketch and turned it into something ten billion times more amazing. (Yes, she does take commissions!) If you’re wondering, certain items on our shelves do have special meanings; can you guess the reasons behind them??

We’re using a (heavily) customized version of the Delicate theme by NattyWP; the overall design and all graphics other than the header are by Paola, with loads of input and well-timed moral support from Alix. We’re also excited to report that we’re now powered by the Ultimate Better Book Blogger plugin! Still working on the transition, but it’s already so helpful!

Giveaway

Because it’s our birthday, Alix and I have decided to give away some books that are near and dear to our hearts. We’ll be choosing two winners (open internationally); one randomly via Rafflecopter, and one handpicked from the comments. We’ll send the winners a book of their choice from our selection below. As always, please be sure to read our giveaway policy before entering. Good luck, everyone!

Alix’s Picks:

  • Attachments by Rainbow Rowell (Goodreads) – Attachments is the debut novel by Rainbow Rowell who is also the author of Eleanor & Park and the very much anticipated (at least by Paola & I ) upcoming novel Fangirl! The story is about a guy who falls in love with a coworker via her emails to her friends (his job is to read emails flagged as inappropriate by their company email system). But this triggers within himself a journey of self-reflection, accepting and change. Â Attachments is one of my all-time favourite books because it heartwarming, delightful, gut punching and just simply storytelling at its best. Donât be fooled by the chick lit classification because while yes the story centers around a love story, it is so much more than that. The main character is a guy, which is so refreshing to me, and a huge nerd.
  • The Sea of Tranquility by Katja Millay (Goodreads | Alix’s Review) – I read The Sea of Tranquility a few months ago and posted a review here AND I AM STILL NOT OVER THIS BOOK. TSOT is everything powerful YA should be. The main characters were beautifully broken and wonderfully developed. The story flowed consistently and never felt over the top and self pitying all while dealing with issues of severe trauma, post-traumatic stress, depression and death. While this book is not necessary a happy book, it is a book about optimism, perseverance and the resilience of humans. I cannot boast enough about it.
  • Ready Player One by Ernest Cline (Goodreads) – Ready Player One is another book that appears to be one thing but is really something else. Set in a futuristic world where most people spend their time inside a virtual world, the story follows an unlikely hero who must complete challenges in order to win a coveted prize. This book is overflowing with 80s reference and it is amazing! Any kid who was a old enough to be aware of pop culture from the 80s/early 90s will be delighted off the bat! This is also another story that follows a male geek hero on a journey of growth and so much more (I guess I love those books?). Thereâs action, thereâs love, thereâs real consequences to your choices have horrible results. I laughed, cried and gasp while ready it and I loved every word I spent with it.

Paola’s Picks:

  • The Darkest Minds by Alexandra Bracken (Goodreads | Paola’s Review) – I knew immediately that I wanted to give away a copy of this book. Beware: it’s a Certified FBF (Facepunched By Feels) book that will leave you on the brink of tears by the time it’s over.
  • Golden by Jessi Kirby (Goodreads) – I haven’t published my review of this one yet, but I will say right now that I read it practically in one sitting. Part coming of age story, part tantalizing mystery, I think it’s one of those books that will work for you whether you regularly read contemporary or not.
  • The Night Circus by Erin Morgenstern (Goodreads | Paola’s Review) – I had to think really hard about my final giveaway choice, which was ridiculous because DUH. It had to be this one! Still among the most beautiful, magical, unforgettable stories I’ve ever read. If you haven’t experienced it yet, you need to. Trust me.
