Kindred Spirits: Friendships Through Blogging

January 20th, 2014 | Posted by Paola in The Reading Life | Thoughts & Musings

So, I have a pretty exciting social life. No joke — I’m invited to TONS of parties every month. Um, children’s parties, anyway. I am extremely popular with four-year-olds. Their parents tend to like me too. And does dinner with my boss count? Cause I do that like twice a year.

… Fine. I do NOT have a very exciting social life. Don’t judge!

I’m not sure if this is true for everyone, but for me, it’s gotten increasingly harder to make new friends as I get older. I’ve always moved around a lot since I grew up in a military family and then married into the military as well, but back then I could meet people through school, and as an adult I could also meet them at work. I’m now in my late twenties, married, with no kids… and I mainly hang out with preschoolers. (Who are, btw, awesome.) Since I’m 26 going on 46, I don’t really do anything that’s considered “fun” by most members of my generation. It’s difficult to find people to befriend as a result, but something that has been true about me and my social life since forever is that I’ve never had an easy time meeting people who love to read as much as I do. Until now, that is!

âKindred spirits are not so scarce as I used to think. It’s splendid to find out there are so many of them in the world.â

– Anne of Green Gables by L.M. Montgomery

I’ve said it before, but I’ll say it again: the best thing to come out of my decision to return to blogging (mostly) full time is the amount of friendships I’ve made through it. For the first time in my life, a vast majority of the people I call friends actually share my passion for books. I’ve met so many awesome, intelligent, funny, classy, unforgettable friends over the past year of being part of the book blogging community and it NEVER ceases to amaze. Some of them I’ve been lucky enough to meet in person. Others, I have sworn an undying vow to meet in person someday. We constantly force reading material on each other, never mind the teetering TBR piles. We shriek, flail, keysmash and capslock our way through books that deliver massive facepunches to the feels. (Or, feelspunches. Speaking of which, we invent new vocabulary on a daily basis.) We talk about fictional people as if they were sitting right next to us. We bond over a love for stories and imaginary worlds. I suddenly have so many kindred spirits.

It’s kind of the best thing ever.

But it’s not something I’ve always had, and lately it’s gotten me wondering just why it’s so hard to find other book lovers in real life. It seems like most people I’ve encountered offline are the type who read because they have to, or who occasionally read for pleasure but never quite feel the same way I do about a good book. They ask polite questions about what I’m reading, or express fleeting interest but usually move on to other conversation topics. Meanwhile, I have this huge network of bookworm friends online who have made books such a fundamental part of their lives. What’s going on here?? And also, where have you guys been all my life???

What about you? Do you have a lot of friends offline who love reading and books? Or do you find them all via book blogs, like me?
