Love Overdue

August 28th, 2013 | Posted by Alix in Review | Romance

TITLE: Love Overdue

AUTHOR: Pamela Morsi

GENRE: Romance

RELEASE DATE: August 27, 2013, Harlequin

SOURCE: Digital ARC (Netgalley)

Buttoned-up book lover DJ is all sensible shoes, drab skirts and studious glasses. After an ill-advised spring-break-fueled fling left her mortified, she’s committed to her prim and proper look. When she’s hired by a rural library in middle-of-nowhere Kansas, she finally has the lifestyle to match-and she can’t wait to get her admin on.Â But it’s clear from day one that the small-town library is more interested in circulating rumors than books. DJ has to organize her unloved library, win over oddball employees and avoid her flamboyant landlady’s attempts to set her up with the town pharmacist. Especially that last part-because it turns out handsome Scott Sanderson is her old vacation fling! She is not sure whether to be relieved or offended when he doesn’t seem to recognize her. But with every meeting, DJ finds herself secretly wondering what it would be like to take off her glasses, unpin her bun and reveal the inner vixen she’s been hiding from everyone-including herself. (from GoodReads)

Overall I thought the story was OK. I don’t know much about rural libraries and wheat farming but the way the story explained and addressed seemed to ring true to me. I actually found the details the book provided about harvest time and how it rallies and affect small farming communities to be really interesting! I thought that plus the “make the library better!” stories made an original backdrop to the main romance storyline.

The story of the woman that leaves the “big city” to find herself is a common enough trope in romances these days and Love Overdue very much uses it. I could understand why DJ wanted to move to small town and get the change to be in charge of a library at a young age. I did like the small town aspect of the story and the eclectic cast of characters that make up it’s residents. I think the setting of the story was my favourite part!

I Â liked both DJ & Scott as the romantic leads. I do wish that DJ had been less of the clichÃ©d “repressed, stodgy, conservation” librarian type. I did like though that Scott was happy to be living in a small town and was the “reluctant hero who must run the family business but wishes he lived elsewhere). I enjoyed the tension between DJ and Scott through out the story. I thought their encounters were cute and I was enjoying DJ’s inner narration/outrage at Scott not remembering that she was the girl he had a fling with 8 years ago during spring break! I was really excited for them to get together and was looking forward to seeing how they would deal with the “spring fling” reveal. Unfortunately it ended up being a let down! I was excited and hoping that DJ revealing to Scott that she was his “fantasy” girl would be done in a original manner and through the story in a new interesting directions leading to these characters growing close. But instead the book did something (I don’t want to be too spoilery here) that made me feel like it was an easy way out and overall an epic let down after all that build up. I did find it pretty ridiculous that Scott wouldn’t recognize DJ after 8 years (while she did) but had suspend my disbelief and was able to go along with it looking forward to how they would handle that revelation but instead of giving us that, we get NOTHING. NADA. ZIP. ZERO.

My other major issue with Love Overdue is the character of Viv, Scott’s mom. I get the the meddling mom (or other family member) is another common trope in romance but it was taken wayyyy to far here. Viv crosses the line into creepy territory even after it’s revealed that she hired a private investigator to find a librarian to match her son up with! She takes the whole meddling mom to a whole new level!! And there was the disturbing element introduced half way through that implied that Viv wanted Scott to find love cause she was dying but then we get the shock of realizing that no, she wanted him to find love so he would be settle and SHE COULD KILL HERSELF AND JOIN HER RECENTLY DECEASED HUSBAND. I’m fine with heavier elements being addressed in romances but it was poorly done here and very disturbing. Even more so that after DJ’s dog stops her from being able to do so, the chapter ends, we get one last chapter of DJ and Scott and then the epilogue where Viv is apparently no longer depressed and happily alive with a boyfriend. Why even bother introducing such a heavy subject if you’re just gonna sweep it under the rug? UGH.

RATING: 1.5/5

Love Overdue just didn’t do it for me. I wanted more romance and found that I was ultimately cheated of experiencing the characters truly come together. The “meddling mother” trope was also played up too much for my liking and choosing to address such a heavy topic a depression/suicide without giving it the proper attention was just something I couldn’t dismiss.

I received a free copy of this book in exchange for an honest review. Many thanks to Harlequin and Netgalley for the ARC!
