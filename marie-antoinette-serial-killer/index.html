Marie Antoinette, Serial Killer

Before I get into the story, can we take a moment to talk about the cover? This was another case of attractive cover pulling in me. IÂ love it!

Marie Antoinette, Serial Killer was a fun read for me. I ended up reading most of the book in one sitting, which is always a good sign! The storyÂ is centred around Colette, a American teenage student going to Paris for the first time. Colette started off to me as being a rich bitch. The way she treated her brother and mother, the sense of extreme teenager emoness resulting from her parent’s divorce and resulting less comfortable financial situation made me wonder how I was going to be able to get through the book. Hannah and Pilar, Colette’s rich BFFs were also quite insufferable. Basically think poor man’s mean girls. Hannah I really disliked. She was very disagreeable and not even in a enjoyable way. I was kind of hoping she would be one of the victims… Luckily about half way through the book (coincidentally when I found myself reading the rest of it in one sitting), Colette goes through character development that made her much more sympathetic to me. Once she finally starts to change and realize that being a “mean girl” isn’t what it’s all cracked up to be, I definitely liked her a lot more. I also have to say that I did understand why she wanted to badly to be in with Hannah and Pilar. I think the reality is that most of us, given the opportunity in high school, would most likely chose to hang out with the rich, popular girls if it made the HS more “easy”. I would have liked more time to spent on her character development how realizing it might appear to be easier to be a mean girl, it isn’t all that it’s cracked up to be and not the person she wanted to be.

I’ve never been to Paris but still I felt like I could picture all the places Colette visited. The Catacombs were claustrophobic, the descriptions of the Eiffel Tower at night made easily picture and overall I just felt like the book did a good job of bringing Colette’s Paris to life.

I really liked Colette exploring the mystery meaning of the symbol on an old pendant she found in a box from her French grandmother that she keeps seeing all over Paris. I though the discovery of what it meant and how it worked with the serial killer mystery was really interesting. I wish there had been more about that and devilling into the her family’s past and connection to Marie Antoinette. I also enjoyed the whole ghost serial killer story. I found it was fun to have a few pages told from the perspective of the characters who are about to murdered. It attended more intrigued and mystery to the story to me and made me want to keep reading.

There’s also a small romance in the story between Colette and their teenage tour guide. It wasn’t anything special truth be told. I wasn’t like ~swoon! over them. It was cute but honestly, if there hadn’t been a romance, it wouldn’t changed anything really for me.

The biggest issue I had with the book was just how quickly the whole “murdering ghost on a revenge path” plot was resolved. I don’t want to spoil it without getting too much into it but I really liked the build up to the climax and then boom! it’s over. It was just way too easy and quick for me and didn’t live up to what the rest of the book built up. The book is relatively short and I feel like the way this plot line was resolved is a result of that, much Â like not enough time being spent on Colette’s character development.

RATING: 3/5

Marie Antoinette, Serial Killer was a quick and fun read. I really enjoyed Colette’s Paris and found the serial killer plot line, despite it’s ultimate pitfall, interesting.

A big thank you to Scholastic for providing us with an ARC via NetGalley in exchange of an honest review!
