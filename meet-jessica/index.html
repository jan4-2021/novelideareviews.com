#2. Oliver Twist by Charles Dickens

Like I said before, I really love the classics. Oliver Twist was probably my very first classic novel. I had no choice but to fall in love with it. Oliver is an orphan boy who is just trying to survive the rough streets of London. He meets some very interesting people along the way like, Jack Dawkins (The Artful Dodger). I really like Oliver as a character, which is strange because I usually really love complex characters, since I can often relate to them, but Oliver is not complex. He does not battle with good and evil, he is just pure and innocent, and I think that is why I really like this book because, Oliver did nothing wrong, he was just dealt a bad hand. I appreciate Oliver because he could have just lived the life he had be given, but instead he ran away from problems and tried to make life better, which worked for his benefit because in the end that’s exactly what he got, a better life.

Jessica

JESSICA is a 23 year old housewife/dog mom. She is a southern belle living in Las Vegas, NV, for now. Her hobbies include painting, drinking sweet tea, reading, and dancing. Almost every major life decision she has made has been based on the result of a Pro-Con list. Her two favorite authors are Charles Dickens and Sarah Dessen, and she carries a copy of The Catcher in the Rye with her wherever she goes. Jessica is completely obsessed with Robert Downey Jr. and Gilmore Girls. Her main goals in life are to relocate her train of thought (derailed sometime in 1998) and to make better homemade biscuits than her mom.
