Month In Review: August 2013

August 31st, 2013 | Posted by Paola in Month in Review

Hereâs a handy roundup of everything we reviewed in August! This is a feature that weâll post on the last day of every month just to reflect on the best, worst, and generally most memorable book moments we had. Itâs also the new home of our monthly book haul as a replacement for Stacking the Shelves!

We reviewed 10 books this month! That is by far the lowest number we’ve turned out so far, but I’m totally okay with it. Things have gotten so unbelievably hectic for us that we had to consciously make the decision to slam on the brakes! I honestly didn’t fully realize that we were, in fact, posting pretty much every single day of the week. This, my friends, is exhausting. But the thing is, we have enough content that we could continue to post every day of the week through most of September. This kind of crazy pace takes its toll, so Alix and I are still planning on slowing down over the next few months as we wrap up 2013. We have a ton of ARCs in September (somehow, ALL THE BOOKS are releasing in September?!) and slightly less in October, but by November we’ll be able to breathe. Whew!

August was a special month for us because we celebrated our 5th birthday! We unveiled a shiny new redesign and also hosted a reader’s choice giveaway that just ended earlier this week. Thanks again to all of you for your support of our blog and all the amazing friendships we’ve made through A Novel Idea. You guys mean so much to us!

Day 1 | Day 2 | Day 3 | Wrap-Up

I had a blast collaborating with Charlotte at Gypsy Reviews (she’s my good friend and book twin) on a three-day event called Far East Reads, which was our way of celebrating our Asian heritage but also an opportunity to talk about the certified badass who is Mulan. We did a chat review of the incredible Shadows on the Moon by Zoe Marriott, PLUS had the honor of interviewing author Kay Honeyman, who wrote The Fire Horse Girl (one of our most favorite books ever). The event was a blast to co-host, and we were just thrilled by the amazing response we got from readers. Thanks everyone! More collabs between our blogs are on the horizon! Stay tuned!! (Or be very afraid, whichever~)

Oh, and don’t miss my recap of the Crown of Midnight launch party, during which I got to meet my idol Sarah J Maas AND dissolve into a mess of fangirly feels with the lovely Charlene at Bookish Whimsy! BEST. DAY. EVER. Even though I’m pretty sure Stacie at Fairybook Mommies went home thinking I was insane hahaha /hides

By the way, earlier in August I announced a project with Tory at The Sleeping Latte called Round Robin Writing! There are now four of us working together on what may be the most ridiculously epic fairytale retelling you have ever seen. We’re still in the planning stages but our goal is to start sharing chapters around December, if not earlier! GET READY FOR THE AWESOME.
