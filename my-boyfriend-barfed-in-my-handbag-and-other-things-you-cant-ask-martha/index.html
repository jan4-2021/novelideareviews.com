THIS BOOK. IS HILARIOUS. Like I can’t even describe to you how funny it is, not to mention so so so so SO relevant to my daily existence. I’m definitely nowhere near being domesticated in any sense, and the very idea of cleaning is enough to completely demotivate me from getting out of bed in the morning. Uhh, that’s probably why my husband was staring at me when I was so excited about the opportunity to absorb Jolie Kerr’s infinite wisdom — I mean, it would be more logical for me to run away from this book screaming bloody murder. But the thing is, if you’re as much of a cleaning-phobe as I am, then YOU WILL LOVE THIS BOOK. And also, you need it. Desperately. Because it will make cleaning actually sound… /GASP/… kind of fun. And hysterical.

Likewise, if you looooooove cleaning and it’s your favorite thing ever in the whole world, you’re gonna love this book too. Seriously. It’s for everyone. I firmly believe this.

My love story with this book began in the table of contents, when I saw that the very first chapter was entitled, “The Kitchen: Clean It, or Just Set It On Fire And Be Done?” EXCUSE ME, BUT IS THIS CHAPTER DEDICATED TO ME??? And as I continued on my adventure in learning about how to clean ALL the things, it became evident that I will never be able to live without all this knowledge ever again. The author sets it all down for you in plain terms, but with a sense of humor that had me laughing nonstop. She had me laughing nonstop about topics like the 10 trillion uses for white vinegar, for crying out loud. I learned that you shouldn’t put fabric softener sheets in the dryer with your towels. I now know everything these is to know about the vermin that could possibly invade your house (thanks to a handy table in which mice are described as ‘scurrying, furry, beady-eyed hell demons’) and how to get rid of them. I have immense amounts of knowledge concerning how to rid my house of ALL THE HAIR.

This is the cleaning how-to book for people like me who somehow entered adulthood without any domestic senses whatsoever. I don’ t even care how lame I sound right now; the truth is that cleaning my house is one of my absolute least favorite grown-up obligations. But thanks to this amazing lady, I feel a lot better about it.

And you know, even if you don’t think you need to be schooled in the fine art of cleaning, you should read this book just because it’ll make you laugh until your face hurts. Jolie Kerr fills every chapter with anecdotes and personal misadventures, and she delivers everything with so much wit. It’s kinda like sitting down for some Real Talk with the Fairy Godmother of All Clean Things. She’s funny, she’s understanding, and she knows when you need some tough love about the laundry disaster waiting for you upstairs. The chapters are never boring. You’ll be amazed at how riveted you are by all this talk of Mr Clean Magic Erasers (THEY REALLY ARE MADE OF MAGIC), why mops are gross, and when/how to stop weeping inconsolably cause you spilled nail polish all over the table (guilty as charged errrr).

I’d gush some more, but I think you just need to get a copy. And buy your best friend a copy too. Hell, buy everyone a copy. We could have a cleaner, funnier world!!!

RATING: 5/5
