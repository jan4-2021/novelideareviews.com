Recap: Crown of Midnight Launch (Or, EEEEK)

August 29th, 2013 | Posted by Paola in Events

I paused to take a photo in between hugging the book and sobbing. You should thank me.

OKAY, EVERYONE CALM DOWN NOW. SERIOUSLY. Oh, it’s just me still walking around squeaking incoherently and having to take deep yoga breaths every few minutes? Ah. Okay. Well, fine then. I guess I’ll pull myself together and write this post now. Errr.

Ahem. SO! On Tuesday afternoon I embarked on a 3 hour and 48 minute drive from Las Vegas, NV to La Verne, CA so I could be there for the Crown of Midnight launch party at Mrs. Nelson’s Books. I can’t even articulate how ridiculously excited I was for this to happen. I mean, I’ve been writhing in agony and pretty much dying to get my hands on the book for months now. (Some of you may still remember the tragic moment when I was rejected for the ARC on Netgalley and subsequently spiraled into despair for days afterward — especially since the rejection was horrifically timed right before my birthday… *starts crying again just remembering this*)

But wait! There were two other huge, hysteria-inducing reasons for my excitement: 1) of course, Sarah J. Maas was going to be there and I MIGHT ACTUALLY GET TO SPEAK WITH HERÂ  and 2) one of my most fabulous bookish friends, Charlene from Bookish Whimsy, was going to be there too! Um, yeah, indulge in a mini freak-out with me real fast.

Our giant pile of books waiting to be signed (with Finnikin thrown in for good measure haha)

Needless to say, from the moment I arrived at the bookstore I was in a state of pure, undeniable, all caps EEEEEEKKKK. I’m not kidding. My brain was just one long string of EEEEKKKKKK for pretty much the entire night. It went a lot like this:

  • I found Charlene in the bookstore and we hugged and flailed around — EEEEKKKK
  • There was chocolate cake (Charlene got to it ASAP, guys. Don’t mess with this girl and the cake.)Â  — EEEEEKKKK
  • I picked up a gorgeous hardcover copy of Crown of Midnight — EEEEEEKKK
  • I realized that fellow blogger & recent Twitter acquaintance Stacie was also there — EEEEEEKKKK
  • Sarah J. Maas arrived and was waving to everyone — EEEEEKKKKKK

Charlene & I :D

And so on, et al etc, for like 3 hours. The level of Awesomeness only escalated while Sarah was talking about how she got published and the story behind the Throne of Glass series. Right off the bat she mentioned both Robin McKinley AND Garth Nix as inspirations while she was dreaming of becoming a published writer from the time she was in high school — that’s right, two of my absolute favorite authors of all time. (Just picture me in my seat flipping out and essentially wanting to wave a sign saying SARAH WE ARE KINDRED SPIRITS.) She went on to answer some questions about her upcoming New Adult series, scheduled for 2015 publication, which isÂ  going to be a fantasy based on Beauty and the Beast, East of the Sun, West of the Moon and Tam Lin. (I already knew about the series coming out, but picture me freaking out over it anyway at this point.)

Then, someone raised their hand and asked Sarah what kind of books she reads when she isn’t busy writing. This, my friends, is when the levels of Awesomeness completely transcended all previous possible limits, because Sarah answered that she’s a huge Melina Marchetta fan. She told us about meeting Melina at Comic-Con last year and just being utterly overwhelmed by the experience, all while Charlene and I were high-fiving and essentially dying of joy in the third row. Sarah confessed that she actually hadn’t even started the third book yet because she wasn’t sure she could handle the feels, and I couldn’t resist chiming in that we were in the same boat. This kind of derailed the rest of the conversation as we veered into Finnikin fangirling territory. It went something like this:

Us: “OMG WE HAVEN’T READ QUINTANA OF CHARYN YET EITHER. We decided to read it together for the moral support!!”

Sarah: “OMG YOU GUYS ARE MY SPIRIT ANIMALS”

Us: “OMG HELP THAT JUST MADE MY ENTIRE LIFE EEEEKKKK”

The rest of the room: …..?????

Guys. I just. I can’t even. Askahfdkjahsdfkjhakshdfkjsdhf.

We were the last to get our books signed, which was probably a good thing since this process took some time to get through. Sarah was amazing throughout the entire book launch, by the way. I didn’t think I could love this author any more than I already do, but I LOVE HER SO MUCH. It was my first ever book signing and I’m so glad this is the author I got to see. She was super funny, fangirled over Finnikin with us, and patiently signed everything we’d brought. She also wrote in our copies of Finnikin of the Rock (all the while saying SORRY MELINA!!! and squeaking while saying she couldn’t believe she was “defacing” a Melina Marchetta book hahaha) and took a photo with us. BEST DAY EVER AAAHHHH.

She had to sign her name really small on my copy of Throne of Glass because we were both saying EEEEE and she wrote it all over the page before signing!

It was such an incredible experience. Seriously. I feel like I could babble on and on about it forever. Sarah was already a favorite author of mine and it was almost overwhelming getting to meet her and finding out how warm, approachable, and AMAZING she is in person. Plus I got to meet Charlene!!!! AAAHHH!!! The funny thing is that even though we’d never actually met before, I already felt like we’d known each other forever because of Twitter and email and our blogs. So it was a lot like seeing an old friend. I laughed so much my face hurt.

LOL my glasses were falling off my face and you can’t see my eyes!! I look like this: ^__^ OH WELL IT DOESN’T MATTER BECAUSE IT WAS THE BEST DAY EVER

THANK YOU SO MUCH SARAH!!!

Head over to Bookish Whimsy today to check out Charlene’s recap of the event, which will probably be much more dignified than mine, haha. You should be immensely jealous that I got to talk about cake and The Little Mermaid with her!!! I just finished Crown of Midnight yesterday (I went on a total binge and accomplished nothing else) so watch for my review coming soon. In the meantime, I wrote a guest post for the Throne of Glass tribute over at Ink Shelves, which will be up on their blog this coming Sunday! You can go hear me wax poetic about Celaena Sardothien, who is MY spirit animal. (Well, one of them. The other is Isaboe of Lumatere, obviously.)
