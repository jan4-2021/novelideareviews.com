Recaps or Forecasts?

So for a few months now we’ve been trying to post both an end of the month recap AND a monthly forecast telling you about upcoming highlights on the blog. The truth is, doing both is rather exhausting, even when they only have to be done once a month. Alix and I both work full time and it can be difficult to work these in, especially when the book haul can be a pretty big post. We may only keep one of these posts a month, but we’d like to ask our readers which you prefer: do you like recaps of what we’ve been up to all month, or do you prefer to know what’s coming up in the future?? Please leave us a comment or hit us up on Twitter to let us know your thoughts!

New Feature: Classically Challenged (!)

Somehow, my insane recaps of Persuasion by Jane Austen (something we did for Classics Retold back in September) turned out to be some of our most popular posts on the blog. Because I reeeaalllyyy want to read Across a Star-Swept Sea by Diana Peterfreund but I have to get through the original story first. Watch for the recaps every Friday this month! July 2013 Favorites

Paola’s Pick:

The Darkest Minds (The Darkest Minds, #1) by Alexandra Bracken

While it may seem like I cry my face off during practically every book I read, the truth is that very few are able to get that kind of reaction from me. (I’m not actually that big of a crybaby, I swear. Err…) The Darkest Minds is definitely the kind of book that needs to have a big neon warning label on the cover so that everyone who decides to read it will be well aware of its high FBF (Facepunched By Feels) Factor. I’m so relieved that I read it just days before the e-novella was released, and even more relieved that I was approved for Never Fade on Netgalley. I honestly have no idea how I would be coping right now otherwise. That ending left me reeling! (Even though I saw it coming! UGH) Another awesome thing: Alexandra Bracken is on the list of YA authors coming to Las Vegas in November for Vegas Valley Book Festival, so I get to see her in person and I AM SO EXCITED. I highly recommend this one! Read my slightly incoherent review over here.

July 2013 Book Haul

July 6th was our 20th and final Stacking the Shelves post. After some deliberation and discussing it with some fellow bloggers, we decided that it was time for us to think of a new way to handle our book haul posts. While being part of the STS meme introduced us to many awesome people, it also meant a lot of meaningless comments from others who seemed to just be copy-pasting the same thing (“HEY I’VE NEVER HEARD OF ANY OF THESE BUT COME VISIT MY STS HERE -INSERT LINK-”) every week. Quality is more important than quantity, at least for us, so our book hauls are now being posted once a month.
