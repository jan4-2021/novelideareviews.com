Kindred Spirits: Friendships Through Blogging

January 20th, 2014 | Posted by Paola in The Reading Life | Thoughts & Musings - (28 Comments)

So, I have a pretty exciting social life. No joke — I’m invited to TONS of parties every month. Um, children’s parties, anyway. I am extremely popular with four-year-olds. Their parents tend to like me too. And does dinner with my boss count? Cause I do that like twice a year.

… Fine. I do NOT have a very exciting social life. Don’t judge!

I’m not sure if this is true for everyone, but for me, it’s gotten increasingly harder to make new friends as I get older. I’ve always moved around a lot since I grew up in a military family and then married into the military as well, but back then I could meet people through school, and as an adult I could also meet them at work. I’m now in my late twenties, married, with no kids… and I mainly hang out with preschoolers. (Who are, btw, awesome.) Since I’m 26 going on 46, I don’t really do anything that’s considered “fun” by most members of my generation. It’s difficult to find people to befriend as a result, but something that has been true about me and my social life since forever is that I’ve never had an easy time meeting people who love to read as much as I do. Until now, that is!

The Lure of the Last Page

June 25th, 2013 | Posted by Paola in The Reading Life | Thoughts & Musings - (16 Comments)

I’ve never been very good with surprises. I’m a planner — I like calendars, schedules, agendas, spreadsheets, and timetables. I make lists constantly, both to stay organized and to soothe this inner need for order. The ten billion things I need to do on any given day are suddenly so much more manageable when they’re written down in neat rows. And I usually have a fairly good idea of what my day has in store, but I’m already anticipating the next day long before that one is over. Others believe that bridges should be crossed when you come to them. I, on the other hand, can already see the bridge looming on the horizon and want to know what’s happening after we cross it.

Which probably sounds pretty tiresome and possibly downright terrible to some of you, but I am strangely at peace with this part of my personality. It’s not that I can’t handle the random things life throws my way, because of course not everything is predictable, and who would want it to be? You might not be able to believe it after that last paragraph, but I’m actually pretty good at improvising. I just, err, don’t like to wing it all the time. Surprises make me kind of uncomfortable. That’s why I’m often guilty of flipping to the last page of a book just to peek at the ending.
