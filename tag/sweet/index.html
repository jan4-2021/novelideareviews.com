Chime

February 25th, 2013 | Posted by Paola in Fantasy | YA Fiction - (2 Comments)

TITLE: Chime

AUTHOR: Franny Billingsley

FORMAT: eBook, 298 pages

I’m being an idiot and trying to write this review too soon after finishing the book. I’m always really inarticulate and incoherent right after reading something I loved, so bear with me. I will try to extol to you the many virtues of Chime and its odd, clever, funny, headstrong, heroine. Or anti-heroine. I’m sure she would have a stronger inclination towards the latter.

Stepmother warned Briony never to tell father what happened. He wouldn’t understand, she said, and of course that was true. If father knew how wicked Briony was, how truly depraved, things would not go well for her. And it wouldn’t just be father who would turn on her. In the Swampsea, right on the edge of a bog filled with all manner of eerie fey creatures, no one would take kindly to the truth that Briony is a witch. Witches are hanged in the square for all to see. Thus, Briony keeps silent, and she stays away from the swamp. Stepmother warned her about that too. But there’s no way for stepmother to protect her now, because she is dead. Briony goes on living her life, which seems to darken daily as she contemplates all the things she must atone for. There’s her sister Rose, known in their village for being “not quite right in the head.” There’s the father she never speaks to if she can help it. And of course there are the memories: twisted, fragmented memories of what really happened the night Stepmother died. The truth is buried deep, but the arrival of a boy named Eldric and a threat that encroaches on the person Briony has sworn to protect might just be enough to bring it all to light.
