Okay so… I have a new goal in life. Travel to Savannah, Georgia, meet the Taylor’s and be adopted into their family of crazy awesome powerful witches. This book was great! I loved all of it. Spellcaster

April 29th, 2013 | Posted by Paola in Contemporary | Fantasy | YA Fiction - (2 Comments)

TITLE: Spellcaster

SERIES: Spellcaster, #1

AUTHOR: Claudia Gray

GENRE: YA Fantasy/Contemporary

FORMAT: Hardcover, 370 Pages

Nadia can feel that something just isn’t right about her new town. It’s a dark, terrible magic that lingers over Captive’s Sound like an unending storm. Descended from a line of witches, Nadia tries to figure out where this darkness is coming from and why it’s there in the first place. This leads her to a boy named Mateo, who in turn has inherited a gift of his own: the ability to see the future in his dreams. And that future is even darker than the sinister enchantment eating away at Captive’s Sound. Bound together by magic and prepared to fight what fate holds in store for them, Nadia and Mateo work to unravel a web of secrets that can be traced back over the centuries to one desperate choice and the descent into madness that followed.

This was an underwhelming read for me. From the synopsis, I guess I was expecting something more. At the very least, it seemed like there wouldn’t be an aggravating love triangle to deal with. And no, there isn’t a love triangle in this book, but then again there’s not much of anything else going on either. Everything that could potentially have been compelling always seemed to fall short, and neither the characters nor their relationships with one another ever felt fully realized. Despite some interesting ideas and lovely imagery, I walked away from Spellcaster feeling disappointed.

Chime

February 25th, 2013 | Posted by Paola in Fantasy | YA Fiction - (2 Comments)

TITLE: Chime

AUTHOR: Franny Billingsley

FORMAT: eBook, 298 pages

I’m being an idiot and trying to write this review too soon after finishing the book. I’m always really inarticulate and incoherent right after reading something I loved, so bear with me. I will try to extol to you the many virtues of Chime and its odd, clever, funny, headstrong, heroine. Or anti-heroine. I’m sure she would have a stronger inclination towards the latter.

Stepmother warned Briony never to tell father what happened. He wouldn’t understand, she said, and of course that was true. If father knew how wicked Briony was, how truly depraved, things would not go well for her. And it wouldn’t just be father who would turn on her. In the Swampsea, right on the edge of a bog filled with all manner of eerie fey creatures, no one would take kindly to the truth that Briony is a witch. Witches are hanged in the square for all to see. Thus, Briony keeps silent, and she stays away from the swamp. Stepmother warned her about that too. But there’s no way for stepmother to protect her now, because she is dead. Briony goes on living her life, which seems to darken daily as she contemplates all the things she must atone for. There’s her sister Rose, known in their village for being “not quite right in the head.” There’s the father she never speaks to if she can help it. And of course there are the memories: twisted, fragmented memories of what really happened the night Stepmother died. The truth is buried deep, but the arrival of a boy named Eldric and a threat that encroaches on the person Briony has sworn to protect might just be enough to bring it all to light.
