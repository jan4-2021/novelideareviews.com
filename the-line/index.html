Okay so… I have a new goal in life. Travel to Savannah, Georgia, meet the Taylor’s and be adopted into their family of crazy awesome powerful witches. This book was great! I loved all of it. The main reason I loved it… It was written by a man and told from the perspective of a female. Usually, men capture the wrong essence of females in novels… We are not always whining and complaining, sometimes we are deceitful and mysterious, and sometimes we are honest and loving. That’s why this book is so great because it literally takes you through every emotional battle a woman can have. Not to mention, who doesn’t love a book about a bunch of Sassy powerful Southern witches?

So one of my favorite things about the book was the world that J. D. Horn created. Every little description about Savannah could very easily be visualized. Sometimes when I’m reading books, I have a hard time visualizing the world that the book takes place in… But in The Line that was not the case. I also really loved how the characters in the book referred to Savannah as almost a living thing not just the city. Like “she”, Savannah, had a magical essence of her own. And I just really love that the town wasn’t just a setting, it was part of the story, it was alive with the story.

Another thing I really enjoyed about this book is that you’re not on the fantasy side of the fence. The main character, Mercy, has no magical powers whatsoever, while her twin sister Maisie, was born with more power than all the witches in their family. It’s like we’re on the outside looking in the window, but it’s exactly the same way Mercy feels. In fact Mercy even describes herself as a fish flopping on dryland while the river flows right next to her.

The characters in this book are all very interesting. From the main character of the story to characters that have very small parts, all of them were interesting. Mercy’s family has kept her in the dark about a lot of things she should have known about, and once she starts finding everything out, the characters get more complex and you either love or hate them.

The last thing that I really just loved about this story is that I could only predict one thing that was about to happen. It was one of those things that right before it happened you knew it was going to, but what you didn’t know was how all the other characters were going to react. Sometimes when you predict events in the book you can often predict the reaction of the characters. In this book that was not the case, I could not predict the reaction of the characters because the characters were so well-written, and because we were in the mind of Mercy we didn’t know how any of the other characters were going to react when when it happened. But every other prediction I thought I was right on, I was completely wrong about it.

I will say that the love triangle was a bit of an annoyance in the beginning but the good news is is that it’s easily avoided throughout the book and it kind of resolves itself. It’s not one of those love triangles that you have to put a lot of energy into trying to figure out who she’s going to choose, it just works itself out in the end which is something I really appreciated, because love triangles stress me out sometimes.

RATING: 4/5

Overall, this book was wonderful. It grabbed my attention right away, and kept it throughout the entire book. The characters and the world were very wonderfully created. And even though some characters did things that should have been unforgivable… I could very easily relate to Mercy’s ability to forgive and let live.
