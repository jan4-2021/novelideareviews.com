The Lure of the Last Page

June 25th, 2013 | Posted by Paola in The Reading Life | Thoughts & Musings

I’ve never been very good with surprises. I’m a planner — I like calendars, schedules, agendas, spreadsheets, and timetables. I make lists constantly, both to stay organized and to soothe this inner need for order. The ten billion things I need to do on any given day are suddenly so much more manageable when they’re written down in neat rows. And I usually have a fairly good idea of what my day has in store, but I’m already anticipating the next day long before that one is over. Others believe that bridges should be crossed when you come to them. I, on the other hand, can already see the bridge looming on the horizon and want to know what’s happening after we cross it.

Which probably sounds pretty tiresome and possibly downright terrible to some of you, but I am strangely at peace with this part of my personality. It’s not that I can’t handle the random things life throws my way, because of course not everything is predictable, and who would want it to be? You might not be able to believe it after that last paragraph, but I’m actually pretty good at improvising. I just, err, don’t like to wing it all the time. Surprises make me kind of uncomfortable. That’s why I’m often guilty of flipping to the last page of a book just to peek at the ending.

Now, this doesn’t happen all the time. It has to be a book that I love, usually, with characters that I’m extremely attached to. It’s particularly impossible to resist when it’s the final book in a series and I JUST HAVE TO KNOW. Is my favorite character going to survive?? Do these two end up together after all?? DOES THIS SHIT REALLY GO DOWN??? The thing is, I just can’t rest easy until I know. (I realize that sounds neurotic hahaha /shuffles away) I mean, I legit CANNOT focus on the story or the plot if I’m anxious about something/someone in the story. But the moment I turn to the last page and skim for clues, and the instant I get even vague answers to my questions, I feel better. I can stop worrying and go back to reading. Otherwise, I speed through the narrative in need of near-instantaneous closure.

I’m guilty of doing this, but I don’t feel guilty about it. I guess I’d feel more guilty about not really absorbing much of a book because I was so hellbent on reaching the ending. They do say it’s the journey that matters. So I will continue to flip to the last page if the burning need comes over me, and if you do it too, then now you know you’re not alone!

What about you? Do you ever flip to the last page of a book to peek at the ending? Or do you have WAY more willpower than I do?? (Bonus points if you recognize the book in my photo above!)
