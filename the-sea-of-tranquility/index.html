I finished it about a week ago (after reading it in one sitting!), and the emotions it caused in me still feel fresh and truthfully I am still processing them. Can you tell I loved it? Because I think saying I loved this book is an understatement.

Nastya Kashinkov is a strong but broken teenager. Hoping to escape her past, she moves in with her aunt and starts at a new school. The story is told from the POV of Nastya and Josh, something that works incredibly well here. I really enjoyed getting each character’s perspective and in the case of Nastya, it showed the duality of just how strong she is while also being deeply affected by the past events.

Nastya as a character is depressing but also amazing. She clearly suffering from the result of a traumatic event: she chooses not to speak, to beÂ invisibleÂ yet also puts herself out there in aÂ confrontationalÂ manner with how she dresses. Josh Bennett, the other lead of the story and Nastya’s love interest, is an equally broken character, albeit in a different. Everyone is Josh’s life has left him and now he just wants to shut down and be invisible. But that changes with the arrival of Nastya. The dynamic between Josh and Nastya is captivating. The give and take, push and pull, had me turning page after page. I just couldn’t get enough of them. And this went beyond just them falling for each other. Alix is in her late twenties, a project manager by day and bookworm-in-training by night. She has always been a avid reader, receiving awards from her high school for having taken out of the most books. Alix loves reading in English and French. She will sometimes pick up a French book in its English translation if the cover is prettier (she also really likes pretty things) and has her bookshleves organized by book cover colour. A fan of all things British (the royalty, Downtown Abbey, tea, Colin Firth), Alix will forever hold Jane Austen in a special place in her heart as her novels were the first works of classic English fiction she read as in impressionable teenager. Alix is mostly reading YA (enough with the love triangles already!) and romance novels (a genre people are often too quick to dismiss) these days.
