Tumble & Fall

September 18th, 2013 | Posted by Alix in ARC | Contemporary | Review | YA Fiction

I was attracted to this title because its pretty cover and catching summary. I love stories sent against an on-coming/occurring apocalypse (I have watched The Day After TomorrowÂ an almost embarrassing number of times)Â soÂ Tumble & FallÂ seemed right up my alley!

The story follows 3 teens living in Martha’s Vineyard who are each dealing with heavy emotional baggage in the wake of an asteroid about to hit Earth. Sienna, who is a “summer resident”, has just been taken out of a hospice by her dad where she was being treated for mental issues. Her father brings her to their summer home in the Vineyard, which they haven’t been to since her mother’s death years before, in hopes of making their possibly final days there happy. Once there, Sienna happily reunites with her younger brother but is shocked to find out that her father not only has a girlfriend but they plan to marry before the asteroid hits. Zan is an island native who has been struggling with the untimely death of her boyfriend over one year ago. As the end nears, Zan discovers that he might not have been the man she thought he was and start to question the premise of their relationship. Caden, another island native, comes from a broken home with an alcoholic mother and father who walked on them when he was a kid and never came back. Until now. Caden finds himself kidnaped by his father and forced to finally deal with his issues regarding his father and family.

I liked the premise of 3 separate story lines told by 3 different characters. I felt that while the characters weren’t connected beyond living their possibly final days in the same community, the 3 stories still related to each other since they were all dealing in one way or another with the same issues: abandonment and family.

I struggled with the selfishness of the Sienna and Zan. I understand that as teenagers they would put love in front of family but it was never completely justified in a way that felt right with me. Sienna basically falls in love and abandons her family within a couple of days and Zan, despite what might be the end of the world fast approaching, cannot see beyond her broken heart to be with her family (I’m being a little harsh here since the character arcs does that these characters come to their senses eventually but it was all a little too late to me). That feeling of not completely understanding or being able to relate to the characters was something I struggled with for the whole book.

I thought Sienna was the most interesting character out of the 3 teens because she was the one who was the most broken (yes I also really like broken characters). I understood the motivation behind her actions the most because I felt like we got the most progression out of her inner monologued instead of the same repetition of things with Zan and Caden. I didn’t agree with her just abandoning her father and brother for some random guy she’s apparently in love with. But the story did a good job of explaining the issues she struggles with, being afraid of failing her father and brother, pushing them away and her fear of the depression she suffers from taking ahold of her in the same way it did her mother, that pushed her towards making that choice and then her subsequent ones that lead up to the end of her story.

I didn’t like some of the choices that were made with Caden story. I know Caden’s father is meant to be portrayed as centred, controlling, a-hole but I really didn’t understand why he suddenly orders a prostitute to take his son’s virginity! Like seriously? WHY. How does he even know Caden is a virgin?!?! Just all of that encounter and resulting inner narration by Caden confrontation with his dad felt awkward and off in a really bad way. I understand that there needed to be something that happens to have Caden decide once and for all to cut ties with his add but I found the introduction of this virgin/prostitute storyline jarring and unnecessary. I really wish the author had made a different choice here. Oh and I also could have lived with Caden’s crush on his kinda-step-mother and everything that happened with her. It was a total clichÃ© if you know what I mean.

Zan I found to be… boring. I never connected to her story. I thought she was whiny. I found the story about her half-sister to be way more interesting then her dead boyfriend’s but it took a back burner. I don’t really have much more to say about her. I also had a hard believing that it would be that easy for Zan to travel to Boston and back within that short of timeframe considering the oncoming asteroid.

My biggest issue with the book was that while it was character driven, the characters didn’t drive enough the story to make it captivating for me. I kept reading the book mostly because I still loved the premise and kept waiting be enthralled, for more to happen, to have one of the stories jump out at me. Yet it never happened. It eventually started to feel boring. There was a slowness to the pacing that didn’t work to me and while there was somewhat sense of oncoming doom, I didn’t find it prominent enough considering. I wanted to have that sense of the world might be about to end but unless one of the characters mentioned it or talked about the presidential speeches, I forgot more then once the larger context of the book.

RATING: 2.5/5

Tumble & Fall was just an okay read for me. I was left feeling unsatisfied by the lack of captivating characters and the end didn’t work for me. The book had great potential and I don’t regret reading it. It just unfortunately not ended up being a memorable read.
