Wild Cards

October 16th, 2013 | Posted by Alix in Review | YA Fiction

TITLE: Wild Cards

AUTHOR: Simone Elkeles

GENRE: YA

PUBLISHED: October 1st 2013; Walker Children’s Books (Bloomsbury USA Children’s Books)

From some reason I’ve been on a sport theme book kick recently. I don’t know why since I’m not a sports person (I don’t follow any sports per say and only watch the occasional hockey game here and there) yet this is the 3rd book centre around a sports player I’ve read in as many months!

Derek is your classic good boy gone bad. He was a star athlete and great son until the death of his mom, which results in him choosing to basically not care about anything anymore. Now living with his ditzy mother and having issues with his absentee dad (who’s in the Navy), Derek’s life is turned upside again when a school prank leads to his expulsion and move to a suburb of Illinois. Ashtyn on the other hand, is the classic good girl with major issues. Her mom abandoned her family when she was a kid and subsequently her older sister Brandi (Derek’s stepmom), also. Finding herself living with only her father, who is stuck in his own bitter world, Ashtyn has found joy in playing on her high school’s football team.

The characters were very much classic clichÃ©s: Derek the bad body, Ashtyn the good girl, Landon the asshole boyfriend, Brandi the ditzy stepmom and Gus the bitter absentee father. I wish the story had pushed these characters to have more depth and complexities. There’s nothing necessarily wrong with characters that have clichÃ©s traits but for me, it made them lack originality and depth. I wanted to know more about Brandi and what led to her leaving her sister and father behind. How did she meet Derek’s dad? I wanted more about Derek’s dad as well. I liked the peek we got in Derek’s parents romance but it wouldn’t have been nice to get more depth in how their moving around affected Derek. There was plenty of missed potential there.

What I did like was that Ashtyn is a female football player! That was definitely original and I love how she was already a fully integrated member of her HS team. There wasn’t any issues with her male teammates and I loved how they had her back and stuck up for her. The dynamic of her and teammates as well as her being a football player were all parts of the story I really liked.

The story really lagged for me. The bulk of the book was so slow moving and very predictable. Of course Derek and Ashtyn clash. Of course Ashtyn’s boyfriend is a jerk (I also saw the twist about him coming a mile away). Everything was just a set up to Derek and Ashtyn yet it was so slow and repetitive, I made me not as interested in them as I could have been. Speaking of which:Â HOW IS THE FACT THAT THEY ARE STEP SIBLINGS NOT REALLY AN ISSUE FOR ANYONE?! Not their families or friends or any other character for that matter bring up the fact that Derek and Ashtyn are related as ever being weird or you know, could make their relationship THAT MORE COMPLICATED. But nope! Seriously, the fact that they are step-aunt and step-nephews was really just an “easy” way of having them live together and be forced to meet/interact. I really wish the story had addressed this. I was fine with the romance between them, after all they aren’t related. But one of the things that made me pick up the book was my curiosity of how they would deal with technically being related. I was so disappointed that this ended up pretty much being swept under the rug.

The story becomes rushed for the last 50 pages are so. Derek’s goes to see his Grandmother, who starts off as a manipulative, judgemental character who suddenly does a 180. I wish there had been more character development here cause it came off super quick and out-of-character to me to see Derek not only bend to his Grandmother’s will so easily (after being told it’s what his “mother would have wanted”) and on the flip side, “Grams” uprooting her whole life and being OK with Derek/Ashtyn out of, what felt like, nowhere. Derek and his Grandmother’s relationship could have worked but it was just too rushed to make sense to me.

RATING: 2/5

Wild CardsÂ is a book with great potential but for me, it ended up being problematic. The premise of a female football player, who is ambitious and unapologetic about her love of the playing the game and life goals is great and was by far my favourite about the book but it wasn’t enough to make it work as a whole.

Thank you to Bloomsbury USA Children’s Books from providing us with an ARC via Netgalley!
